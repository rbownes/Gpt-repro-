## What actually moved the number

### 1. Effective batch size — the dominant lever (−0.485 bpb)

Starting `GRAD_ACCUM=32` with `MICRO_BATCH=16` gave an effective batch of **512 sequences** (≈524 k tokens/optim-step) and only ≈112 optimizer steps in 300 s. The model was compute-fed but update-starved.

Systematically halving `GRAD_ACCUM`:

| GRAD_ACCUM | eff batch (seqs) | optim steps | val_bpb | Δ |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 32 | 512 | 112 | 1.805 | — |
| 16 | 256 | 219 | 1.602 | −0.197 |
| 8  | 128 | 446 | 1.467 | −0.135 |
| 4  |  64 | 889 | 1.402 | −0.065 |
| 2  |  32 | 1752 | 1.319 | −0.083 |
| 1  |  16 | 3433 | 1.333 | +0.014 (elbow crossed) |

Same kernels, same tok/s (≈193 k), but 16× more weight updates — a far better loss at the same wall-clock. The elbow sits at batch-32: smaller loses to gradient noise.

### 2. Regime change flipped every other optimum

The batch-size move invalidated all prior hyperparameter tuning. What was correct at batch=512 was wrong at batch=32:

| Knob | Best at batch=512 | Best at batch=32 | Why the flip |
|---|---|---|---|
| `BETA2` | 0.95 | **0.9995** | Smaller batch → noisier gradients → longer variance EMA helps. |
| `MIN_LR_RATIO` | 1.0 (constant LR) | **0.3** (cosine decay) | With 1700+ steps, annealing pays off; with 110 steps it didn't. |
| `PEAK_LR` | 5e-4 | **9e-4** | Cosine decay lets us run a hotter peak without instability. |
| `WARMUP_FRAC` | 0.02 | **0.04** | Higher peak LR needs proportionally more warmup. |
| `GRAD_CLIP` | 1.0 | **0.5** | Noisier grads benefit from tighter clipping. |
| `WEIGHT_DECAY` | 0.1 | **0.0** | Still under-fit even at 1760 steps; regularisation is a net cost. |

The `BETA2` move alone was −0.024 bpb across four incremental keeps (0.95 → 0.98 → 0.99 → 0.995 → 0.999 → 0.9995). The `PEAK_LR`/`MIN_LR_RATIO`/`WARMUP_FRAC` co-tuning added another ≈−0.025.

### 3. Things that looked plausible and didn't help

- **`WARMUP_FRAC` shortening** (every batch regime): warmup is cheap insurance, not overhead.
- **`MICRO_BATCH=32` / `GRAD_ACCUM=1`** at the same effective batch: bigger shapes went to 26 GB VRAM and slowed tok/s.
- **`sdpa_cudnn` / `sdpa_efficient`** attention backends: within noise of `sdpa_flash` on this workload.
- **Pre-compile warmup step before the timer**: `torch.compile` is already disk-cached; the "free" first step just ran at PEAK_LR before LR-warmup and destabilised init (+0.074 bpb). A cautionary tale — `tok_per_sec_warm` was already compile-free in the reported numbers.
- **Reducing per-step CPU-GPU sync** (divergence check every 50 steps): saved 0.3 % of steps, swamped by noise.
- **`torch.compile(mode="max-autotune")`** (full CUDA graphs): crashed with `accessing tensor output of CUDAGraphs that has been overwritten` during backward. The U-Net skip connections share `wte` output across blocks, which breaks cudagraph output tracking even with `cudagraph_mark_step_begin()`.

## Final configuration

```python
MICRO_BATCH     = 16
GRAD_ACCUM      = 2              # effective batch = 32 seqs = 32 k tokens
PEAK_LR         = 9e-4
MIN_LR_RATIO    = 0.3            # cosine decay to 30 % of peak
WEIGHT_DECAY    = 0.0
BETA1           = 0.9
BETA2           = 0.9995
EPS             = 1e-8
GRAD_CLIP       = 0.5
WARMUP_FRAC     = 0.04           # ≈12 s of the 300 s budget
COMPILE_MODE    = "max-autotune-no-cudagraphs"
ATTENTION_BACKEND = "sdpa_flash"
```

Throughput: ≈193 k tok/s (unchanged from baseline), peak VRAM 14.3 GB, ≈1760 optim steps per 300 s run.

## Takeaways for future Phase-A work

1. **Batch size was the single most impactful variable on this hardware/budget.** All other tuning was <0.03 bpb per change. Worth re-checking first whenever anything structural shifts (budget, model size, GPU).
2. **Hyperparameter optima are batch-size-dependent.** After a big batch-size move, re-sweep everything even if it was "already tuned". `BETA2=0.95` was confidently optimal before the move and clearly wrong after.
3. **Kernel-level speed didn't move bpb.** `sdpa_cudnn`, compile modes, sync-reduction, bigger micro-batches — all within noise or worse. At 194 k tok/s, the GPU is already near-saturated on this workload; the cheap wins were on the optimisation side.
4. **Watch noise.** Single-run val_bpb jitter is ≈±0.005 at this budget. Several "keep" decisions here are within that band and should be treated as provisional until re-run with multiple seeds.
5. **Disk-cached `torch.compile`** means `training_seconds` ≈ `total_seconds` after the first ever run. Any optimisation predicated on amortising compile cost is moot in the measurement regime.
